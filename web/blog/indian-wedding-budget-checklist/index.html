Indian Wedding Budget Checklist

Indian weddings often span multiple ceremonies and involve both families, which makes a written Indian wedding budget planner essential. Use this checklist to make sure no category is forgotten before you start booking.

Pre-wedding rituals

  • Engagement ceremony venue and catering
  • Mehendi/Sangeet setup, music, and decor
  • Haldi ceremony essentials

Venue & catering

  • Hall or banquet booking with advance and balance due dates
  • Catering cost per plate, including any guest count buffer
  • Stage, mandap, and entrance decor

Attire & jewellery

  • Bridal and groom outfits for each ceremony
  • Family attire
  • Gold and jewellery sets

Photography, gifts & logistics

  • Pre-wedding shoot, candid photography, and videography
  • Return gifts and gifts exchanged between families
  • Guest travel and accommodation
  • Invitations, printed or digital

Track it as you book

As each item is confirmed, log the total cost, advance paid, and pending balance in a marriage budget planner so the family always knows where the budget stands. The free calculator on this site gives you a starting split across these categories.

Frequently asked questions

What categories should an Indian wedding budget cover?

An Indian wedding budget typically covers pre-wedding rituals (engagement, mehendi, sangeet, haldi), venue & catering, attire & jewellery, and photography, gifts & logistics including guest travel and invitations.

What is often forgotten when budgeting for an Indian wedding?

Pre-wedding ceremony costs (mehendi, sangeet, haldi), guest travel and accommodation, and a miscellaneous buffer for last-minute expenses are the items most often left out of an initial wedding budget.

How do I track Indian wedding expenses as I book vendors?

Log the total cost, advance paid, and pending balance for each item as it's confirmed in a marriage budget planner, so the family always knows where the budget stands against this checklist.
