What is a marriage budget planner?

A marriage budget planner is a structured way to decide how much a wedding should cost, break that total down into categories, and track real spending against the plan as bookings happen. Instead of scattered notes, screenshots, and WhatsApp messages between families, a proper wedding budget planner keeps every expense, payment, and pending balance in one place.

Whether you're organising a large Indian wedding budget planner with multiple ceremonies, or a more intimate Kerala wedding budget planner with specific local traditions and vendor costs, the same core idea applies: set a budget per category, log actual costs as they're confirmed, and always know what's still owed.

Wedding expense categories

Most marriage budgets break down into a similar set of categories. Track each one separately so overspending in one area doesn't quietly eat into another.

Venue & catering

Hall booking, catering per plate, decoration of the venue, and staffing.

Clothing & decor

Bridal and groom outfits, family attire, mandap and stage decor.

Jewellery & gifts

Gold, jewellery sets, and gifts exchanged between families.

Photography & video

Pre-wedding shoots, candid photography, and videography packages.

Invitations & travel

Printed or digital invites, guest travel, and accommodation.

Miscellaneous buffer

A contingency for last-minute costs — most planners keep around 10%.

Paid vs pending tracker

The most common reason wedding budgets go off track isn't overspending — it's losing sight of pending balances across many vendors. A good wedding expense tracker shows, for every booking, the total cost, the amount already paid, and the balance due, along with the date it's owed.

Shared family contributions

Most Indian and Kerala weddings split costs between the bride's family, the groom's family, and the couple themselves. A reliable marriage expense calculator should let you tag each payment by who contributed, so reconciling who paid for what stays transparent instead of becoming an awkward conversation after the wedding.

Export to PDF/Excel

Once your budget is finalised, you'll usually want to share it with family or a wedding planner. Exporting the full expense list — categories, paid amounts, pending balances, and contributions — to PDF for printing or Excel/CSV for further analysis keeps everyone aligned on the same numbers.

Frequently asked questions

What is a marriage budget planner?

A marriage budget planner is a tool that helps couples and families set a total wedding budget, split it across categories like venue, catering, jewellery and decor, and track how much has been paid versus how much is still pending.

How do I calculate my wedding budget?

Start with your total available funds from both families, list every expense category (venue, catering, attire, jewellery, photography, travel, gifts), assign a percentage or fixed amount to each, then track actual spending against that plan as bookings and payments happen.

Is there a free Indian wedding budget planner?

Yes. The calculator on this page is free to use, and the Wedding360 – Planner & Budget app offers a full marriage expense tracker with categories, paid vs pending status, and shared contributions at no cost to get started.

How do I track paid and pending wedding expenses?

Record every expense with its total amount, what has actually been paid, and the due date for the balance. A good wedding expense tracker shows the pending amount per category and overall, so nothing is missed before the wedding date.

Can I track contributions from both families separately?

Yes. A shared marriage budget planner should let you log who paid for what — the bride's family, groom's family, or the couple — so contributions and reimbursements stay transparent and easy to reconcile.

Can I export my wedding budget to PDF or Excel?

Yes. Wedding budgets are easiest to share with family when they can be exported to PDF for printing or Excel/CSV for further calculations, so everyone involved can review the same numbers.
